A. Glossary of AI Terms Relevant to Resume Tailoring: Speak Fluent Robot

Alright, tech wizards, it's time to expand your vocabulary. Memorize these terms and you'll be chatting up ATS systems like a native speaker in no time.

Your AI Lingo Cheat Sheet:

  1. Natural Language Processing (NLP): The AI equivalent of learning to speak "human." It's how computers understand your resume without getting a headache.
  2. Machine Learning (ML): Imagine if your computer could learn from its mistakes. Now imagine if it could learn from your mistakes. That's ML!
  3. Applicant Tracking System (ATS): The digital bouncer of the job application nightclub. Your resume better be on the list.
  4. Sentiment Analysis: AI's attempt at understanding human emotions. It's like giving a robot a mood ring.
  5. Named Entity Recognition (NER): The AI version of "Where's Waldo?" but for important information in your resume.
  6. Semantic Search: When keyword matching goes to grad school and learns about context.
  7. Predictive Analytics: AI's crystal ball for your career. Less mystical, more mathematical.
  8. Deep Learning: When AI decides that simple machine learning is too mainstream and goes hipster.
  9. Optical Character Recognition (OCR): Teaching computers to read handwriting.
  10. Generative AI: The AI equivalent of a creative writing course. Sometimes brilliant, sometimes... well, let's just say it's still learning.

STEM Pro Tip: Impress and Confuse

Casually drop these terms into conversation at your next networking event. Watch as eyes either light up with recognition or glaze over in confusion. Either way, you'll stand out.
